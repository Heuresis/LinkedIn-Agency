# Playbook — Hybrid Full-Stack Growth

> The primary playbook for this workspace's archetype. Combines outbound DM operations (for revenue-now) with inbound content compounding (for the long-runway audience asset). Designed for operators who need cash flow in Q1 AND a defensible audience by Year 2.

## When to use this playbook

This is the default playbook unless operator context overrides:
- Operator runway: ≥6 months OR willing to fund the runway from outbound-driven revenue
- Operator willing to be public face of the agency
- Operator can dedicate 25-35 hrs/week to combined ops (with VA support)
- Vertical addressable audience on LinkedIn ≥50K
- Operator has at minimum 1 validated case study OR a credible "doing-the-work-publicly" thesis

## The three-phase arc

```
Phase 1 (Months 1-4):    Outbound-led survival + content seeding
Phase 2 (Months 5-12):   Hybrid balance + audience compounding
Phase 3 (Months 13-24+): Inbound-led + program tier launch
```

The phases are not gates — they're shifts in resource allocation as the operator's pipeline composition changes.

---

## Phase 1: Months 1-4 — Outbound-led survival + content seeding

**North star.** Land 3-5 validated case-study clients via outbound. Seed the content engine. Don't expect content to convert yet.

**Resource allocation.**
- 60% outbound DM ops (volume work, calls, closes, fulfillment)
- 25% content creation (5x/week posts, founder voice)
- 10% foundation work (voice extraction, ICP refinement)
- 5% systems / encoding (filling profiles, capturing patterns)

### Week 1-2 — Foundations sprint

Run in this order, no skipping:

1. `/extract-founder-voice` (agency-voice). Get to ≥80% before any content ships.
2. `/build-icp` (agency-icp). Get to ≥75% before any DMs go out.
3. `/design-retainer-offer`. The first offer doc, even if pricing is uncertain — it gates the application form.
4. `/build-application`. The qualification gate behind the offer.
5. `/build-outbound-engine`. The Cold + (eventually) Liked-post + Comment-for-magnet sequences.

### Week 3-4 — Outbound launch

- Source first lead list (200-500 contacts matching ICP).
- Launch Cold sequence at 25-40 sends/day, ramp to 75-100/day by week 4.
- Operator sends M1 personalization manually for first 3 weeks (calibrates the workspace + builds reps).
- Track reply rate, qualified replies, calls booked.
- Target by end of week 4: first 3-5 discovery calls held, 1-2 closed.

### Month 2-4 — Outbound at volume + content seeding

- Outbound to 200-300 cold sends/week, sustained.
- Discovery calls: 6-10/month booked, target 30%+ close rate.
- Sign 2-4 retainer clients in this window.
- Content engine ships 5x/week, founder voice. **Goal: produce content the audience can find later — don't expect inbound from it yet.**
- Pillar mix in this phase: 30% Authority, 30% Personality, 25% Proof, 10% Pier, 5% Offer-bridge. (Light Offer-bridge — no audience to convert yet.)
- Document EVERY closed deal as a proto-case-study. By end of Phase 1, have 3-5 case studies with anonymized transformation data.

### Phase 1 exit criteria

- 3-5 retainer clients signed
- ≥3 validated case studies in `case_studies.agency_case_studies` with `use_in_content: true`
- Content engine producing posts at cadence (even if engagement is low)
- Profile completeness across all 6 profiles ≥60% average
- Operator energy: sustainable (not crashing — if crashing, scope down)

## Phase 2: Months 5-12 — Hybrid balance + audience compounding

**North star.** Convert the content asset into measurable inbound flow. Reduce cold-DM dependency. Build the proof pool that funds Year 2's program tier.

**Resource allocation.**
- 35% outbound DM ops (sustained but scaled-back)
- 35% content creation + engagement (cadence + comment-reply harvest + pier mechanism)
- 20% client fulfillment + voice-drift audits + reporting
- 10% foundation work + systems compounding

### Month 5-7 — The compounding inflection

- Content begins producing inbound DMs (typically starts month 5-7 — varies by vertical and content quality).
- DM-from-content rate: target ≥10/month by month 7, ≥25/month by month 9.
- Pillar mix shifts: 35% Authority, 25% Personality, 25% Proof, 10% Offer-bridge, 5% Pier.
- Outbound DM volume drops to 100-150/week as inbound replaces some volume.
- Operator-led discovery calls now mix inbound + outbound; inbound calls close at higher rate (45-60% vs. outbound 25-40%).

### Month 8-12 — The proof-pool stack

- Sign 2-4 additional retainer clients per quarter via inbound + outbound mix.
- By month 12: 8-12 retainer clients in book, $40K-$120K MRR.
- Case-study density: 8-15 validated, with full transformation metrics, ready for program tier.
- Voice-drift discipline becomes load-bearing: per-client weekly audits run via `/voice-drift-detector`.
- Content engine ships 5-7x/week with comment-reply harvest discipline. Best posts hit 50K-150K impressions.

### Phase 2 exit criteria

- 8-12 retainer clients
- $40K-$120K MRR
- DM-from-content rate ≥25/month (inbound carrying weight)
- ≥8 validated case studies with operator+client sign-off for content + program use
- Operator running 25-30 hrs/week sustainable (not 50+)
- Profile completeness across all 6 profiles ≥75% average

## Phase 3: Months 13-24+ — Inbound-led + program tier launch

**North star.** Launch high-ticket program. Inbound carries 70%+ of new client acquisition. Agency revenue + program revenue compound together.

**Resource allocation.**
- 15% outbound DM ops (only for select high-value targets, no cold volume)
- 30% content + engagement
- 25% client fulfillment (now with team — VA + ghostwriter)
- 20% program delivery (curriculum + cohort calls + community)
- 10% strategic / foundation refresh

### Month 13-15 — Program launch prep

- Run `/design-program-offer` — the high-ticket education tier
- Build curriculum from the 12-15 validated case studies
- Define first cohort size (typically 8-15 students for first cohort, build cohort discipline before scaling)
- Pre-launch content campaign: 30 days of program-positioning content layered into normal pillar mix

### Month 16-18 — First cohort + revenue diversification

- First cohort enrolls (typical: $7K-$15K per student × 8-15 students = $56K-$225K cohort revenue)
- Program delivery: weekly group call + 1:1 mentorship cadence + community
- Track first-win triggers per `/build-first-win-trigger` — first-win rate target ≥70%
- Document program student transformations in real-time for next cohort's proof

### Month 19-24 — Compound engine

- Cohort 2-3 launches (every 4-6 months typical cadence)
- Retainer book stable at 10-15 clients (cap based on team capacity)
- Annual revenue: $700K-$1.5M typical (50-60% retainer + 40-50% program)
- Content engine produces inbound that fills BOTH retainer pipeline AND program pipeline
- Program graduates become referral source for next-tier retainer clients (network compounding)

### Phase 3 exit criteria

- Program tier producing $200K+ annual revenue
- Retainer book stable, gross margin ≥65%
- 70%+ of new client acquisition from inbound
- Operator working 25-35 hrs/week with team support
- Workspace at ≥85% profile completeness across all 6 profiles
- Case-study pool refreshes every quarter (no aging)

## Cross-phase rules

### The "don't skip foundations" rule

In every phase, foundations work continues. Voice profiles get sharper, ICPs refine with closed-deal evidence, content pillars adjust to engagement signal. Operators who skip Phase 2-3 foundations work degrade the encoding and lose Phase 3 leverage.

### The "client-content recursive context" rule

Once any client signs (Phase 1 onward), the per-client subprofiles activate. Every client artifact loads from `brand_voice.client_voices[slug]`, `ideal_customer_profile.client_icps[slug]`, etc. Voice-drift audits run weekly per client. Per `INVARIANTS.md` N-1, N-2, A-6.

### The "case study density" rule

The agency's content + program tier both depend on case-study density. Every closed deal must produce a case study within 90 days OR the deal does not count toward program-launch eligibility. Operators who skip this discipline cannot launch a credible program in Phase 3.

### The "outbound-as-survival, content-as-asset" rule

Outbound is the cash-flow engine. Content is the equity-build engine. Operators who treat them as substitutes (one OR the other) under-perform operators who treat them as complementary (one AND the other in the right resource ratio per phase).

## Common failure modes by phase

| Phase | Failure | Symptom | Fix |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Skipping voice extraction to "start posting" | Content engagement low; calls don't convert; clients churn at month 4 | Halt content ship, re-run `/extract-founder-voice` to ≥80% |
| 1 | Outbound at too-low volume | <2 calls/month; revenue insufficient for runway | Audit M1 personalization; raise daily send target to 75+ |
| 1 | Closing low-margin clients to hit revenue | Margin <55%; capacity-trapped by month 6 | Re-run `/design-retainer-offer` with stricter target_avatar; turn down sub-ICP deals |
| 2 | Content engine plateau (no inbound) | DM-from-content rate stuck at <5/month | Audit pillar mix vs. ICP; rebuild hook library; check for banned vocabulary drift |
| 2 | Voice drift across client roster | Multiple clients comment "doesn't sound like me" | Re-run `/voice-drift-detector` weekly per client; recalibrate ghostwriter |
| 2 | Client churn at month 4-6 across multiple clients | Pattern of cohort 1 clients leaving | Audit onboarding doc, scope guardrails, monthly report quality |
| 3 | Program launched too early | Student first-win rate <50%; refunds in cohort 1 | Pause cohort 2; refresh case studies; sharpen application gate |
| 3 | Operator burnout from over-scope | Energy crash month 16-20 | Hire VA + ghostwriter; cap retainer book; force vacation |
