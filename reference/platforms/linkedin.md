# LinkedIn — Platform Pack

> The deepest of the platform packs. The workspace IS the LinkedIn agency. Every other platform reference (X, IG, YouTube, Email) is downstream of, or feeder to, the LinkedIn engine. This pack documents what LinkedIn rewards in 2026, how the algorithm differs from every other social surface, and how the workspace's skills and agents map to LinkedIn's specific mechanics.

## Why this platform matters (for a B2B LinkedIn agency)

Three structural facts make LinkedIn the only viable primary platform for a B2B services agency at the $5K-$25K retainer tier in 2026.

**1. Audience density.** LinkedIn is the only consumer-scale social platform where the active user base IS the B2B buyer pool. ~900M+ members; ~310M monthly active; the active 310M skews heavily toward decision-makers (~63% with budget authority, per the platform's own ad-targeting filters). On X, Instagram, TikTok, the operator is fishing in a consumer pool and filtering for B2B buyers; on LinkedIn, the entire pool is pre-filtered for professional context. CAC math reflects this — LinkedIn-sourced retainer leads close at ~3-5x the rate of X-sourced or IG-sourced leads for the same offer.

**2. Professional context as priming.** The reader on LinkedIn arrives in "professional content" mindset — they expect business content, evaluate it as professionals, and engage from a buyer-curious posture. The same reader on X or IG arrives in entertainment mindset and resists business content. This priming is worth ~10-15% extra dwell time and ~30-40% higher conversion-to-DM rate compared to identical content on X. The professional-context buffer is the single largest reason hooks can run 7-12 words on LinkedIn vs. 4-9 on X — the reader is already leaning in.

**3. Decision-maker access without paid gate.** The buyers the agency sells to (founders, CMOs, VPs of growth, agency principals) are reachable on LinkedIn organically through content + DM. On nearly every other channel — direct outbound email, conference circuit, paid ads, podcast guesting — reaching the same buyer requires either paid spend or relationship-equity that takes years to build. LinkedIn collapses the access cost to the operator's time and content quality. This is what makes the inbound-content-authority and outbound-dm-operator archetypes economically viable in the first place.

The trade: LinkedIn rewards consistency over a 6-18 month horizon. Operators expecting Week 4 ROI from LinkedIn organic burn out before the platform's compounding kicks in. The platform pack assumes the workspace has internalized this trade — see `reference/operators/external/inbound-content-authority.md` for the runway gap analysis.

## Platform mechanics

LinkedIn's algorithm + UI + audience norms differ from every other social platform on five specific dimensions. The workspace's content engine is engineered against these.

**Algorithm primary lever: dwell time, not engagement velocity.** LinkedIn measures "time on expanded post" as the dominant ranking signal. An impression that produces 1.5 seconds of dwell counts negatively (the algorithm reads "wrong audience"). An impression that produces 12+ seconds of dwell counts strongly positive — distribution lifts immediately. This is why the line-break-every-10-15-words rule and the open-loop-mid-post tactic exist (per `virality-engine.md` mechanism #2). On X, engagement velocity in the first 30 minutes dominates; on LinkedIn, dwell engineering compounded over the first 60 minutes dominates.

**The first-12-impression bet.** LinkedIn runs an explicit early-distribution test. The first ~12 readers determine whether the next ~12K see the post. Engagement (comments > shares > likes) and dwell in the first 60 minutes is the trajectory-locking window. This is the foundation of the comment-reply-harvest tactic, the first-comment-seed tactic, and the publish-time discipline (Tue/Wed/Thu 8-11am operator-local — see `virality-engine.md`). Posts published outside this window cut impressions 40-60% even with identical content.

**Comment depth weighting (1 comment ≈ 12 likes).** LinkedIn weights comment-reply chains far above likes. A comment with a 4-reply thread under it counts as ~4-5x a standalone comment. This is why the workspace's comment-reply-harvest is non-negotiable in the first 60 minutes after publish (per `virality-engine.md` mechanism #3). It is also why the post-CTA matters more than the post-likes — a CTA that produces 8 substantive comments outperforms a CTA that produces 80 likes.

**The pier mechanism (lurking-leverage).** LinkedIn rewards profile-views from external posts. Commenting substantively on a high-engagement post in your subject area pulls profile-views to your account, AND your follow-up post lands in those readers' feeds within 24 hours as continuation of a conversation they already care about (per `virality-engine.md` mechanism #1). No other major platform rewards this lurking-leverage as cleanly — X has a similar but weaker effect via reply-chains; IG has none.

**Mobile-vs-desktop reading split.** ~75-80% of LinkedIn engagement happens on mobile. The "see more" cutoff is at ~210 characters on mobile (vs. ~315 on desktop). This is why hook anatomy is engineered against the mobile cutoff — every hook the workspace ships fits the first ~3 lines of mobile rendering, with the pattern-break in the first line. Posts that look fine on desktop but bury the hook below the mobile fold lose 50%+ of their potential dwell signal.

## Hook anatomy on LinkedIn

The 9 hook formulas in `reference/frameworks/content/hook-anatomy.md` are calibrated to LinkedIn's specific reader expectations and rendering. Per-platform adaptations:

**First-line cutoff = the platform's "see more" boundary.** Mobile (~210 chars) is the binding constraint. The hook formula must complete the pattern-break in the visible first line — never split the pattern-break across the cutoff. The 7-12 word target is engineered to land the entire hook above the cutoff with room for one short pickup line.

**Line-break rhythm.** LinkedIn renders single line-breaks visibly. The mature LinkedIn cadence is one sentence per line, with strategic empty lines for visual rhythm. Walls-of-text are dwell-killers. The hook itself should occupy one line, followed by an empty line, followed by the pickup. This is platform-native; it does not transfer to X (line-cadenced, not paragraph-cadenced) or to email (paragraph-cadenced, no line-breaks-as-rhythm).

**Mobile-vs-desktop divergence.** On desktop, the operator can run a 12-word hook with one specific without the cutoff biting. On mobile, the same 12-word hook may push a number-anchor below the fold. The workspace's default is to write to mobile-first — if the hook works on mobile, it works on desktop; the reverse fails ~30% of the time.

**Per-formula LinkedIn calibrations:**

| Formula | LinkedIn calibration |
|---|---|
| Contrarian-reframe | Works strongest on LinkedIn vs. all other platforms — professional-context buffer makes the reader more receptive to "the ones who win do X instead." On X, the same hook reads as engagement-bait. |
| Specific-stakes | Lossful numbers ("I lost $40K because...") work because LinkedIn audience evaluates the operator as a peer, not as entertainment. Same hook on IG underperforms by 50%+. |
| Named-mechanism | LinkedIn-native — the platform indexes hard on named-IP. "The Recursive ICP Method" reads as proprietary on LinkedIn; on X, the same name reads as gimmicky. |
| Before-after | Works on LinkedIn when the time-stamp is concrete ("18 months ago..."). Works equally on most platforms; this is the most cross-platform-portable formula. |
| List-promise | LinkedIn has a higher tolerance for list-promise hooks than X (where lists feel like content-template) — but lists must be ≤7 items and source-anchored, or they collapse into generic-list theater. |
| Pattern-break | Imperative voice ("Stop doing X") is LinkedIn-acceptable when the operator has earned it via Authority pillar. On X, pattern-break hooks need to be 4-7 words; on LinkedIn, 7-12 with a comma-split is native. |
| Loop-open | LinkedIn rewards narrative loops because dwell time is the lever — readers stay to close the loop. On X, the same loop-open often gets bookmarked-without-read. |
| Question-stake | LinkedIn audience answers questions in comments at higher rates than any other platform — comment depth is a known weight. Limit to ~10% of mix; over-use reads as engagement-bait. |
| Insider-secret | Curiosity-gap hooks work on LinkedIn but decay fastest — by post 5 in a quarter, the audience pattern-matches and engagement collapses. Sparingly. |

## Format library

LinkedIn supports six post types the workspace ships against. The format choice is downstream of pillar (per `pillar-architecture.md` Rule 4) and post-type mapping.

**1. Text post (the workhorse, ~60-70% of mix).** The dominant LinkedIn format. 800-1500 characters for B2B content; 200-500 for personal-story posts; 1500-3000 for proof-pillar case studies. Single-sentence paragraphs, line breaks every 10-15 words, hook in the first line, payoff in the last 200 chars. This is what `/write-linkedin-post` and `/ghostwrite-client-post` produce by default.

**2. Text + image (~10-15% of mix).** A text post with one image (screenshot, chart, photo, simple graphic). Image lifts dwell ~10-20% when relevant — penalizes ~15% when it's a stock photo or filler graphic. Use for: case study screenshots (with permission and anonymization), DM screenshots that prove a thesis, simple data charts, behind-the-scenes photo for Personality pillar. Avoid: stock photography, generic graphics, AI-generated images (LinkedIn audience reads them as low-effort).

**3. Carousel (PDF document upload, ~10-15% of mix).** PDF uploaded as "document" — LinkedIn renders as a swipeable carousel. 5-10 slides typical, max 10. The format excels at: mechanism breakdowns, before-after frameworks, listicles where each item warrants a full slide. Carousels get ~2-3x the dwell of equivalent text posts because each swipe is registered as engagement. The day-2 of the cross-post compound (per `virality-engine.md` mechanism #4) is typically the carousel.

**4. Native video (~5-10% of mix).** 60-180 second video uploaded directly to LinkedIn (not YouTube link). Vertical (9:16) for mobile-first, square (1:1) acceptable. Captions burned in (>80% of LinkedIn video plays without sound). Native video distribution is currently elevated by the algorithm — videos posted weekly compound profile-views faster than text-only schedules. The day-3 of the cross-post compound is the video.

**5. Polls (~2-5% of mix, used surgically).** 4-option polls with 1-7 day duration. Polls drive engagement volume but rarely produce DMs. Use for: ICP signal-gathering ("which of these is your bottleneck right now?"), audience research feeding back into pillar topic clusters, occasional engagement-volume boost. Misuse: polls as content substitute. Limit to ~1 per 2 weeks.

**6. Articles (~rare, situational).** Long-form (1500-5000 words) published as LinkedIn Articles. Distribution is ~10% of equivalent text-post reach, but the article URL becomes a permanent asset that surfaces in search. Use for: cornerstone Authority content the operator wants discoverable later, manifesto pieces, deep-mechanism breakdowns. NOT a primary distribution channel — a permanent-asset channel.

## Posting cadence

Cadence calibrates to operator type and scope.

**Founder/operator (agency-content scope):** 1-2 posts/day, 5-7 days/week. The high end (2x/day) compounds harder but burns content material faster — sustainable only with the discovery-call → content pipeline (per `inbound-content-authority.md`). The low end (1x/day, 5 days/week) is the conservative-sustainable cadence for solo operators who write their own content.

**Ghostwritten clients (client-content scope):** 5-7 posts/week typical. Lower than agency-content because the client's source material refreshes weekly via `/client-content-sourcing-pulse`, and over-posting on a client account drains material faster than the sourcing pulse can refill. 3-5 posts/week is acceptable for clients with thin source material or limited approval bandwidth.

**Phase-calibrated cadence (per `pillar-architecture.md` Rule 2):**
- Phase 1 (audience-building, <5K followers): 5 posts/week minimum. The audience needs frequency to recognize the operator before the algorithm can compound. Below 5x/week, growth flattens.
- Phase 2 (audience-converting, 5K-25K followers): 5-7 posts/week. The compounding kicks in; cadence sustains rather than accelerates growth.
- Phase 3 (audience-monetizing, 25K+ followers): 5-7 posts/week with 1-2 high-effort cross-post-compound days/month. Frequency holds steady; production effort shifts toward depth on the cornerstone posts.

**Publish-time discipline.** Tue/Wed/Thu 8-11am operator-local for primary distribution. Mon and Fri get ~70-80% of mid-week impressions. Sat/Sun get ~30-40%. Off-cycle posting (weekend, late evening) is acceptable for Personality pillar and Pier posts; Authority and Offer-bridge should respect the mid-week morning window.

**Comment-reply block.** Non-negotiable. 60 minutes immediately post-publish, 4-12 hours later for the second wave. Per `virality-engine.md` mechanism #3, this is what locks the trajectory. An operator who publishes and walks away forfeits the algorithmic compounding the post otherwise earns.

## Engagement mechanics

LinkedIn engagement happens through five primitives. The workspace's content + DM funnel is engineered against the conversion path between them.

**Comments (the conversion accelerant).** Comments are where the operator + audience meet and where the algorithm reads engagement-depth. The post → comment-reply-harvest → DM-from-comment path is the highest-converting funnel on the platform. A reader who comments substantively is ~6-10x more likely to convert to a discovery call than a reader who only liked. The workspace's comment-reply discipline (Step 3 below) is calibrated against this conversion lift.

**Shares (the audience-multiplier).** Shares (with commentary) are weighted higher than re-posts (without commentary). A share with the sharer's own commentary surfaces the post to the sharer's network with implicit endorsement. Share rate is a leading indicator of pillar-resonance — if Authority posts share at 3x the rate of Personality posts in a quarter, the audience is signaling the operator's IP is the draw, not the personality.

**DMs (the conversion surface).** DMs are the workspace's primary acquisition surface. The DM funnel splits into four classes per `INVARIANTS.md` N-5 (Cold / Warm / Liked-your-post / Comment-for-lead-magnet) — each class triggers a different sequence per `/classify-dm-intent`. The DM-from-content gate (per `inbound-content-authority.md` Pattern #3) requires every inbound DM to reference specific content; vibe-DMs route to the application form.

**Profile visits (the lurker conversion).** Profile-visit-from-post is the lurker's quiet engagement. Lurkers are 5-10x more numerous than commenters and convert at lower-but-meaningful rates. Profile optimization (header, About section, Featured posts) is the conversion infrastructure for this lurker traffic. Operators who optimize profile copy quarterly see 20-40% lift in DM-from-profile-visit conversion.

**Followers vs. connections.** LinkedIn's dual-graph (follower vs. connection) matters for distribution. Followers see content in feed; connections see content + are reachable via direct DM without invitation. The workspace's preferred posture: open follow (anyone can follow without connection request) + selective connect (operator accepts connection requests only from ICP-matched profiles). This produces large follower count for distribution + clean connection list for warm DM ops.

## Cross-platform repurpose strategy

LinkedIn is almost always the source platform, rarely the destination. The cross-post compound flows OUT of LinkedIn.

**TO LinkedIn from other platforms (rare):** Inbound-to-LinkedIn repurpose happens in two narrow cases. (1) An X thread that landed >5x baseline gets re-shaped into a LinkedIn post — but with full re-engineering: paragraph-cadence restored, 800-1500 char body, full hook formula applied, no thread-numbering carry-over. (2) A YouTube video transcript yields 3-5 LinkedIn posts (one per major mechanism beat). In both cases, the LinkedIn version reads as native — never as "here's my X thread on LinkedIn."

**FROM LinkedIn to X (most common direction):** Top-decile LinkedIn posts (>2x median impressions for the subject) get repurposed to X via `/repurpose-to-x`. Hook tightens 7-12 words → 4-9 words. Body restructures from paragraphs to tweet-per-beat. CTA translates from comment-trigger to bookmark-bait or quote-tweet-bait. ~25% of LinkedIn posts warrant the repurpose; the rest don't compound enough to justify the X audience's attention.

**FROM LinkedIn to IG (case-by-case):** Per `/repurpose-to-ig` — typically only Authority + Proof pillar posts, transformed into carousel-native IG format with heavier visual treatment than the LinkedIn document carousel. The IG audience overlaps less with the LinkedIn audience, so this is true new-reach repurposing rather than reinforcement.

**FROM LinkedIn to YouTube (situational):** Cornerstone posts (the 1-2 per quarter that hit 200K+ impressions) sometimes get expanded into 8-15 minute YouTube videos. The video then becomes the canonical asset and the original LinkedIn post becomes a teaser. This is downstream of the operator's YouTube strategy — not a default repurpose path.

**What carries vs. what doesn't.** The named-mechanism, the specific numbers, the contrarian-thesis, and the operator's voice signature carry across platforms. The line-break rhythm, the comment-reply CTAs, the LinkedIn-context jokes ("the LinkedIn algorithm just changed..."), and the professional-context buffer do not — they need platform-specific replacements per the destination platform pack.

## Account safety

LinkedIn enforces several quiet rate limits and automation policies that, when crossed, produce shadow-bans, account suspensions, or outright bans. The workspace operates well below the limits by default.

**Connection requests.** ~100/week is the unofficial soft cap; ~150/week triggers warnings; >200/week triggers limits or suspension. Workspace default: ≤80/week, sent manually (no automation tool). Operators running outbound DM at scale should ladder warmup over 4-8 weeks rather than ramp instantly.

**DM volume thresholds.** ~50-80 outbound DMs per day to non-connections is the soft cap (unofficial — LinkedIn does not publish). Above this, message-send rates degrade and accounts get flagged. Workspace default for outbound-dm-operator archetype: 30-50 DMs/day, manually sent, with quality gating per `/classify-dm-intent`. Sales Navigator accounts have higher caps but the same automation-detection signals — volume alone doesn't unlock unlimited send.

**Sales Navigator search rate limits.** ~25-30 searches/hour before rate-limiting; ~150-200 saved leads/day before account flagging. Workspace default: stay below half these caps. Per `/source-lead-list`, lead sourcing happens in 2-3 sessions per week, not in a single high-volume burn.

**Automation tool risk (LinkedIn TOS).** LinkedIn's Terms of Service prohibit automated browsing, scraping, and DM-sending. Tools like Phantombuster, Dripify, MeetAlfred, etc. operate against TOS. Detection signals include: predictable timing patterns, high-volume requests outside the LinkedIn app, login from unusual IPs, mismatched user-agent headers. Account bans for automation tool use have risen sharply in 2024-2026. Workspace stance: NO automation tools for content posting, DM sending, or connection requests. Manual ops only. The cost is operator time; the benefit is the agency's primary acquisition asset (the LinkedIn account) survives.

**Content-side suppression triggers.** Posts get shadow-suppressed (impressions cap at ~500-2K instead of expected 10-100K) when they contain: external URLs in body (move to first comment), >2 hashtags (use 0-2), >1 @mention (engagement-bait signal), banned-vocabulary patterns (per `INVARIANTS.md` N-4), engagement-pod activity (per `inbound-content-authority.md` Specific Tactic #5). Per the operator's experience, comment-pod-driven engagement collapses 6-9 months in as LinkedIn's detection compounds — this is a long-fuse account risk, not an immediate one.

**Recovery from suspension.** Suspended accounts can sometimes recover via LinkedIn's appeals process (3-14 day turnaround typical), but the underlying acquisition asset is gone for that window. The workspace's defensive posture: maintain a backup operator account, keep content drafts in `outputs/` (not LinkedIn-only), and treat the platform as rented infrastructure even though it's the primary acquisition channel.

## Common failure modes

Six failure patterns the workspace has guards against. Each maps to a specific INVARIANT or skill gate.

**1. Posting at the wrong time.** Publishing outside Tue/Wed/Thu 8-11am operator-local cuts impressions 40-60% even with identical content. Operators who publish "when they have time" instead of when the algorithm rewards burn ~50% of their potential reach. Guard: `/build-posting-calendar` enforces the publish-time window; calendar template flags off-window slots.

**2. Hook fatigue.** Same hook formula 3 weeks running drives engagement collapse — the audience pattern-matches and stops engaging. Guard: `/build-content-engine` enforces hook-formula rotation in the calendar; `/audit-hook-library-decay` runs quarterly to retire hooks that have decayed >40% from baseline.

**3. Comment-section neglect.** Operator publishes and walks away. The first 60 minutes of comments determine algorithmic momentum (per `virality-engine.md` mechanism #3). Walking away forfeits 30-60% of the post's potential distribution. Guard: comment-reply block is built into the operator's calendar; the workspace's `/run-client-dm-ops` and `/post-approval-tracker` flag missed comment-reply windows.

**4. DM volume overshoot.** Operator (or VA) sends 100+ DMs/day chasing volume. Account gets flagged or suspended. Guard: `/build-outbound-engine` caps daily volume in the engine spec; `/run-client-dm-ops` enforces per-account daily DM caps.

**5. Engagement-pod temptation.** Operator joins a pod in months 4-9 (the demoralizing valley) to fake engagement. 6-9 months later, LinkedIn's detection compounds and engagement collapses. Guard: explicit no-pod commitment in onboarding; `inbound-content-authority.md` Specific Tactic #5 documents the failure pattern.

**6. Voice drift on client accounts.** Ghostwriter slips into agency voice on a client account by post 12; client churns at month 4 with "this doesn't sound like me." Guard: `INVARIANTS.md` N-1 (load client voice), N-2 (run drift detector before ship), N-6 (no agency-client voice contamination), A-12 (weekly drift audit per active client).
