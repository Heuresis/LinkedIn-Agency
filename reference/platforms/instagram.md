# Instagram — Platform Pack

> **Honest framing:** Instagram is a tertiary platform for a B2B LinkedIn agency. The B2B decision-makers this archetype targets — founders, VPs, heads of growth — do not scroll Instagram for vendor evaluation. They scroll it for entertainment, family, friends. Treating IG as a primary growth channel for a B2B LinkedIn agency is a category error that consumes production capacity and returns near-zero pipeline.
>
> **And yet.** A specific subset of operators does extract real ROI from IG: creator-economy agencies (clients are creators who live on IG), design-tooling and design-led B2B (visual proof lands on IG harder than anywhere else), and founder-personality-led service brands where the operator's identity is the offer. For those operators, this pack matters. For everyone else, IG is at best a brand-extension surface, at worst a distraction. Profile honestly.

## Why this CAN matter (for the right operator)

Three specific niches where IG produces measurable inbound for a LinkedIn-agency operator:

1. **Creator-economy agencies.** If the agency's clients are creators (course-builders, info-product founders, personal-brand businesses), those clients live on IG. Agency content that lands on IG meets them in their native habitat. The same Authority post that reaches a B2B founder on LinkedIn reaches a creator on IG.

2. **Design-led / design-tooling B2B.** When the agency's USP includes visual craft (carousel design, brand systems, content production), IG is the demonstration surface. Showing the work is the proof. A LinkedIn-only design agency leaves the strongest visual proof on the table.

3. **Founder-personality-led service brands.** When the operator's personal identity carries the brand (creator-coach, consultant-with-a-following, agency-as-the-operator), IG extends the relationship layer that LinkedIn alone cannot. Stories specifically build the parasocial trust that converts at the program tier.

If the operator does not fit one of these three profiles, IG is optional at best.

## Why this often DOESN'T matter

- **B2B buying decisions don't happen on IG.** A VP of Marketing evaluating a $5K/mo retainer is not making that decision from a reel. They're making it from LinkedIn DMs, agency websites, and discovery calls.
- **The IG audience for B2B content is the wrong audience.** Even if a B2B reel goes viral, the audience that engaged is overwhelmingly other operators, agency-curious creators, and aspiring B2B builders — not the buyer ICP.
- **Production cost is high relative to ROI.** A 10-slide carousel costs 4-6 hours of designer time. A 45-sec reel costs 6-10 hours of shoot + edit. For a B2B agency with sub-1% IG-driven pipeline contribution, that capacity is better spent on LinkedIn content depth or DM ops.
- **The IG algorithm penalizes B2B vocabulary.** Posts heavy with B2B jargon ("retention," "ARR," "GTM motion") underperform on IG even with the right audience present, because the algorithm's natural-language model indexes B2B content as off-platform-norm.

If the agency profile does not flag one of the three "CAN matter" niches, default IG plan is `repurpose-only-for-top-decile-LinkedIn-posts`, not standalone IG strategy.

## Platform mechanics

Instagram in 2026 runs on three primary surfaces with distinct algorithms:

- **Carousel posts.** Save rate is the primary distribution signal. A carousel that hits 8%+ save rate gets re-distributed to non-followers via Explore. Carousels are the highest-leverage format for educational + Authority content.
- **Reels.** Watch-completion rate + share rate drive distribution. The first 3 seconds determine whether the viewer stays. Reels reach the broadest audience but convert at lower intent than carousels.
- **Stories.** 24-hour ephemeral; reach roughly 5-15% of followers per story. Stories build relationship, not reach. Polls + question-stickers drive the highest engagement.

Single-image posts are deprecated in distribution weight — IG has effectively buried them since 2024. For a B2B agency, single-image is rare and only appropriate for personal-moment posts (founder snapshot, team photo, event recap).

The algorithm rewards consistency of format more than consistency of cadence. An account that ships 2 carousels + 1 reel per week consistently outperforms an account that ships random formats at higher volume.

## Hook anatomy adaptations

The 9 hook formulas from `reference/frameworks/content/hook-anatomy.md` apply on IG with one critical difference: **hooks must work visually first, textually second.** A LinkedIn hook lives in 7-12 words of text. An IG hook lives in slide 1 of a carousel or the first 3 seconds of a reel — and the visual carries 60% of the hook weight.

**Carousel slide 1 must do all the hook work.** No "intro" slide. No logo card. No throat-clearing. Slide 1 typography is ≥3x other slides, reads at thumbnail size, and the text alone — without the body slides — must create curiosity strong enough to drive a swipe. The Specific-stakes hook ("I lost $40K because of one onboarding email line") translates well; the Loop-open hook works if the visual matches the tension. The Question-stake hook often fails on slide 1 because IG users don't read questions in feed — they swipe.

**Reel first-3-seconds rule.** The viewer decides at 0:03. If the subject is talking about LinkedIn lead gen at 0:03, the IG viewer scrolls. The hook frame must be: pattern-break visual + 4-7 word overlay + spoken hook line. NO "Hey guys." NO logo intro. Face fills the frame. Audio is muted on most IG views — text overlay is mandatory and must carry the hook on its own.

**B2B jargon kills IG hooks.** "Retention" reads as work; "the email that lost me a $40K client" reads as story. Same idea, different vocabulary. The IG-tone shift in `/repurpose-to-ig` Step 6 handles this — but the vocabulary discipline is non-negotiable.

## Format library

| Format | Length | Use case | Production cost | ROI band |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Carousel** | 8-12 slides (10 default) | Authority breakdown, named mechanism, case study, list-promise | 4-6 hrs designer | High (saves) |
| **Reel** | 15-60 sec (35-55 typical) | Personality moment, sharp Authority take, contrarian Pier | 6-10 hrs shoot + edit | Medium (reach, low intent) |
| **Story** | 24h ephemeral, 15 sec/frame | Promo, behind-scenes, poll, content tease | 15-30 min | Low-medium (relationship) |
| **Single-image** | Static, square or 4:5 | Founder snapshot, team photo, milestone | 30 min | Low (deprecated) |
| **Story Highlight** | Curated story collection | Evergreen FAQ, case studies, offer-bridge | 1 hr per highlight | Medium (utility) |

Carousels are the primary workhorse for a LinkedIn-agency operator on IG. Reels are secondary, used for top-decile Personality posts and high-charge Authority takes. Stories are tertiary and used for promo + relationship layer (program launches, lead-magnet teases, event recaps).

## Posting cadence

The cadence calibrates to the operator's IG strategic role:

- **IG-not-in-mix:** 0 posts. Don't perform IG presence theater. An empty IG account is fine.
- **IG-as-cross-distribution-only:** 2-3 carousels per week (repurposed from top-decile LinkedIn posts via `/repurpose-to-ig`). 1-2 stories per week. No reels unless production capacity allows.
- **IG-in-mix-for-niche-fit:** 3-4 posts per week (mix of 2 carousels + 1 reel + 1 story-cluster). Daily stories.
- **IG-as-secondary-primary (creator-economy / founder-brand):** 5-7 posts per week + daily stories. Treat as second-tier primary, not LinkedIn-equivalent.

For most LinkedIn-agency operators, the right number is 2-3 carousels per week or zero. Anything in between (1 random post per week) signals lethargy and underperforms both options.

Posting time matters less on IG than on LinkedIn. The algorithm distributes over 24-48 hours, not the 60-minute window LinkedIn uses. Consistency-of-day matters more than time-of-day.

## Engagement mechanics

The IG engagement signal hierarchy for a B2B LinkedIn agency context:

- **Saves >>> shares > comments > likes.** Saves are the highest-intent signal — the viewer has decided this carousel is reference-worthy. Save rate above 8% triggers Explore distribution; above 12% indicates the content has hit a deep nerve. Likes are noise; the algorithm has weighted them down significantly since 2024.
- **Comments need substance.** A 3-word comment ("great post!") is weighted near zero. A 15+ word substantive comment is weighted heavily and triggers reply-chain depth. Reply to every substantive comment in the first 60 minutes after publish, same as the LinkedIn first-hour rule.
- **DMs from posts are the conversion signal.** The LinkedIn equivalent of "comment for lead-magnet" works on IG too — comment-trigger CTAs drive DM volume. Auto-DM tools (ManyChat, Inflact) integrate with IG DMs for lead-magnet delivery.
- **Profile-visit-from-post.** IG measures profile visits triggered by a specific post. This is the closest IG equivalent to LinkedIn's profile-view-from-content. Track per post via Insights.

For a B2B agency, the conversion path is: post → save (or watch-through) → profile visit → bio link click OR DM. Optimize the bio link relentlessly — the bio is the IG-side application gate.

## Cross-platform repurpose strategy (LinkedIn → Instagram)

The default IG strategy for this archetype is **repurpose-from-LinkedIn**, not original IG content. The economics work out better and the content is already audience-validated.

**What repurposes well (LinkedIn → IG):**

- **Top-decile LinkedIn carousel posts.** A LinkedIn list-promise post or named-mechanism Authority post repurposes near-1:1 into an IG carousel. The format is structurally identical; only the hook tightens and the typography enlarges. `/repurpose-to-ig` handles this.
- **Personality-pillar moments with strong narrative.** Loop-open story posts on LinkedIn become 35-45 sec face-to-camera reels on IG. The story compounds across formats because emotion translates.
- **Podcast clips.** If the operator has a podcast or YouTube long-form, 30-60 sec extracts work as IG reels. Audio + face is the natural reel format; podcast clips are the lowest-production-cost reels available.
- **Before/after case studies.** Carousel-format on IG with split-frame before/after slides. Saves rate spikes on these because they read as portfolio-worthy proof.

**What does NOT repurpose well (LinkedIn → IG):**

- **Long-form analysis posts.** A 1500-character LinkedIn post that is reasoning-dense doesn't survive 12 slides without massive dilution. Skip.
- **LinkedIn-platform commentary.** A post about LinkedIn algorithm changes lands on LinkedIn; on IG it reads as inside-baseball that the IG audience doesn't care about.
- **Pier posts (controversial-take comments on others' posts).** The value was the LinkedIn comment-section conversation. IG has no equivalent surface — there's no "comment on a competitor's post for distribution" mechanism.
- **Posts with B2B-jargon-heavy hooks.** "How to improve your retention metrics" lands on LinkedIn; on IG it reads as work-content-in-leisure-space and gets scrolled. Either rewrite the hook (per `/repurpose-to-ig` Step 6) or don't repurpose.

Story-as-promo is acceptable: announce a new lead magnet, tease a podcast episode, share a behind-scenes from a client win. Stories are low-stakes; they don't need the production discipline of carousels or reels.

## Account safety / API limits

IG account safety is materially lower-risk than LinkedIn for a B2B agency context, because:

- **No outbound-DM-volume rate limiting at meaningful scale.** A B2B agency on IG isn't running cold-DM volume ops; the LinkedIn DM-volume risk doesn't translate.
- **No "spam content" detection on long-form text.** IG penalizes hashtag inflation and engagement-bait but not B2B carousel content per se.
- **Account suspensions are rare for organic-content accounts.** The risk vector is hashtag stuffing, third-party automation tools that violate IG's API terms (auto-DM bots, auto-follow tools), and copyright violations on reel audio.

API limits worth knowing for tooling:

- **Instagram Graph API (Meta Business):** rate-limited per app + per user. For a typical agency volume (3-7 posts/week, scheduled via Buffer/Later/Metricool), limits don't bind. Heavy DM automation (1000+ DMs/day) does bind and risks account flags.
- **Auto-DM compliance.** ManyChat / MobileMonkey for comment-trigger lead-magnet delivery is IG-API-compliant when used via the official Instagram-for-Business integration. Off-platform tools that scrape IG DMs violate the terms and risk suspension.
- **Reel music licensing.** Trending audio is licensed for personal accounts; business accounts have a smaller licensed pool. Using off-pool music gets reels muted (audio stripped) or pulled.

For a typical LinkedIn-agency IG presence at 2-4 posts per week, account safety is a non-issue. Stay within the official tooling pool and don't automate DMs outside the comment-trigger pattern.

## Workspace integration

IG is integrated into this workspace via three touchpoints:

1. **`/repurpose-to-ig`** — the primary integration. Takes a published LinkedIn post and adapts it to IG carousel or reel format. Pre-flight gates check IG-account context, voice profile, banned vocabulary. Output goes to `outputs/{date}/repurpose/ig/{post-slug}.md`. See `skills/repurpose-to-ig/SKILL.md`.

2. **`/build-content-engine` IG variant (optional).** For operators in the three "CAN matter" niches, `/build-content-engine` can produce a parallel IG pillar set with adapted post-type mapping. Default is to skip the IG variant and rely on repurposing only.

3. **Voice profile contamination guard.** IG content for a client uses `brand_voice.client_voices[client_slug]` with the IG-tone allowance shift (15-20% more casual). Same N-1 / N-6 invariants apply. Voice contamination across LinkedIn → IG is a tracked failure mode.

For most operators in this archetype, integration touchpoint #1 is sufficient. Touchpoint #2 is for the three niches identified above. Touchpoint #3 is invariant-enforcement and runs automatically.

## Common failure modes

1. **Copying the LinkedIn hook directly to IG slide 1.** The LinkedIn hook is 7-12 words optimized for "see more" click. Slide 1 is 6-12 words optimized for swipe AND visual readability AT thumbnail size. A copy-paste hook fails both jobs. Guard: `/repurpose-to-ig` Step 3 enforces slide 1 typography ≥3x and the IG-tightened hook re-runs the score-hook rubric.

2. **Ignoring the visual brief.** Operator ships text-only carousel slides because designer wasn't briefed. Result: slides ship as text-on-color-block, save rate collapses, the carousel underperforms baseline. Guard: Step 7 visual brief is a hard production prerequisite.

3. **Posting B2B-jargon-heavy reels.** "Today I'm going to show you how to improve your B2B SaaS retention metrics" — instant unfollow signal on IG. The vocabulary needs to translate to leisure-space tone. Guard: `/repurpose-to-ig` Step 6 voice fit + banned-vocab regex.

4. **Treating IG as a presence-checkbox.** Operator posts 1 random carousel per month "to maintain presence." The algorithm penalizes inconsistency, the audience doesn't form a content expectation, and the account stalls. Guard: choose 0 OR 2-3+ per week. Nothing in between.

5. **Verbal CTA at end of reel.** IG users skip the last 5 seconds of a reel on first watch. A spoken-only CTA at 0:55 is invisible. Guard: visual CTA frame held 3-5 sec, spoken CTA is supplemental only.

6. **Hashtag inflation chasing reach.** 30 hashtags including "#marketing" and "#business" pulls non-ICP traffic that depresses long-term distribution because the algorithm misclassifies the account's audience. Guard: 3-5 niche hashtags max per post.

7. **Auto-DM tooling with non-compliant providers.** Using a scraping-based DM tool to deliver lead magnets risks account suspension. Guard: ManyChat / official IG-for-Business integrations only.

8. **Treating the IG follower count as a vanity metric without checking conversion.** 50K IG followers + zero discovery calls from IG = the audience is wrong. Guard: track DM-from-IG-content rate monthly. If <2% of inbound discovery calls reference IG, IG is not in the working pipeline.
