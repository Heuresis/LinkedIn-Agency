# Authority — List-Promise + Named-Mechanism — Numbered Principles

> **Source profile:** Founder of a long-form ghostwriting agency serving B2B operators (LinkedIn + thought-leadership content). ~24K followers at time of post. Anonymized per workspace canon.
>
> **Performance signals:** ~71K impressions, ~520 reactions, ~190 comments (high comment volume driven by the trigger CTA at the close), ~38 reshares. Author attributed 28 lead-magnet downloads, 9 discovery calls, and 2 retainer signs ($6K/mo each) to the post over the next 21 days. The follow-up carousel (cross-post compound mechanism) on day 3 added another ~32K impressions and pulled 11 more downloads.

## The post

```
9 principles that turn a generic LinkedIn post into a post buyers screenshot.

The 10th one is the most important — and almost nobody runs it.

ONE: The hook earns the click in 12 words or fewer. If the reader has to expand to find the promise, the post is already dead.

TWO: The first sentence after the hook delivers on the promise. Don't open with throat-clearing. Land the payoff opener.

THREE: Specificity is credibility currency. "$47K in 9 months" lands differently than "a lot of money over time." Use real numbers or don't use numbers.

FOUR: Single-sentence paragraphs. Line breaks every 8-15 words. The mobile reader's thumb is faster than your prose — break for them.

FIVE: One open loop in the middle. "Here's the part most people miss" / "but the real lesson is downstream of that." Loops keep the scroll moving downward.

SIX: Name the mechanism. "The Recursive ICP Method" reads as IP. "A framework I use" reads as commodity. Names compound — every reference reinforces.

SEVEN: One quotable sentence. The line that lives after the post is closed. Screenshot-worthy. Build the post around the line, not the other way around.

EIGHT: The closer lands on a contrast or a prescription. Don't trail off. The last line is the one that gets reread before the reshare.

NINE: The CTA is binary. "Comment 'PLAYBOOK' for the doc" beats "thoughts?" by 12x in measured comment-volume. Specificity wins on the close, too.

TEN: Reply to every comment in the first 60 minutes. This is the algorithmic move that actually decides whether the post hits 6K or 60K impressions. The first nine principles get you the post. Principle 10 gets you the distribution.

I built a 24-page document with the worked examples for each of these — the actual hook templates, the sample paragraphs, the CTA library, and the comment-reply scripts I use for the first 60 minutes after publish.

Comment "POSTS10" and I'll send it.

(Send manually so I can see who's actually shipping content vs. just collecting frameworks.)
```

## Why this worked

### Hook anatomy (per /score-hook 5-axis rubric)

- **Specificity: 5** — "9 principles" sets the exact item count, "post buyers screenshot" sets the exact outcome. Specific number + specific outcome is the list-promise hook's two-axis credibility test.
- **Stake: 4** — the stake is "you're writing posts that don't get screenshotted, and there's a knowable reason why." Mid-stake; not threatening to identity, but threatening to current output quality.
- **Pattern-break: 5** — the second line ("the 10th one is the most important — and almost nobody runs it") is the explicit pattern-break. Reader expected 9 items; the post promises 10 with one held back as the leverage point. This is the loop-open variant of list-promise — the held-item creates a small tension that holds dwell through items 1-9.
- **Voice-fit: 5** — assumes operator-voice fit for prescriptive list format. The "I built a 24-page doc" close requires the operator to actually have the doc (or build it for the post). Voice-fit is high if the operator publishes prescriptive content regularly.
- **Decay-resistance: 3** — list-promise hooks decay faster than stat-bait or contrarian-reframe hooks. The "X principles that..." formula has been heavily worked since 2022. Decay window: 9-15 months in this format before pattern fatigue. Refresh by varying the number (7, 11, 13 work; 5 and 10 are saturated) and varying the qualifier ("principles" vs. "patterns" vs. "moves" vs. "shifts").

### Body construction (mechanism + proof + specificity)

- **Numbered structure (ONE, TWO, THREE...) instead of "1, 2, 3."** Spelled-out numbers slow the eye marginally and increase dwell ~8-12%. Small lift, but compounds across a 10-item list.
- **Each item lands a single specific principle in 1-2 sentences.** No item runs longer than 35 words. Principle + brief mechanism + crisp prescription. The pattern is "claim → why → do this."
- **The held-back item (TEN) is the algorithm move, not a writing move.** Items 1-9 are about the post itself; item 10 is about what happens after publish. This is the pattern-break inside the list — readers expected another writing principle and got an operations principle. The shift is what makes the held-back item feel like the actual leverage.
- **Specific multiplier in item 9** ("12x in measured comment-volume") is the credibility lock. Without one specific number embedded in the list, the post reads as opinion. One number is sufficient to anchor the entire list.
- **The doc-pitch comes after the list, not interspersed.** Selling the lead magnet inside the list breaks the principle-stream and reads as bait. Selling after delivering the full value reads as natural progression.

### Virality mechanism triggered

- **Comment-trigger CTA (mechanism #5 from virality-engine.md).** "Comment 'POSTS10'" drove 190 comments — high relative to the operator's typical 30-50 comment baseline. Comment volume is the leading indicator of whether the trigger CTA worked; this post hit ~4x typical comment volume.
- **Cross-post compound (mechanism #4).** The author shipped a carousel summary on day 3 ("the visual version of the 10 principles") and a 90-second talking-head video on day 5 ("the principle most people skip and why"). The carousel added ~32K impressions; the video added ~18K. Total compound: ~121K impressions across the three formats vs. ~71K standalone.
- **Comment-reply harvest (mechanism #3).** Author replied to every commenter in the first 90 minutes with the doc + a follow-up qualifying question ("which of the 10 are you struggling with most?"). 23 of those reply chains became multi-turn DM conversations.
- **The "send manually" qualification line** (final parenthetical) filters tire-kickers and signals scarcity-of-attention. This pattern caps the magnet-send rate at ~50 in the first 24 hours but lifts the magnet-to-call conversion from typical 8-15% to ~30%. Trade-off: capped scale for higher intent.

### CTA construction

The CTA is **comment-trigger + qualification-in-personality** — the two-layer pattern from file 10 (`offer-bridge/10-hook-loop-cta.md`). Layer 1: "Comment 'POSTS10' and I'll send it." Layer 2: "Send manually so I can see who's actually shipping content vs. just collecting frameworks." Layer 1 drives volume. Layer 2 filters for intent.

The trigger word ("POSTS10") is unique to this post — never reused across posts. Reusing dilutes responsiveness because commenters who saw the prior trigger will re-comment without re-engaging. Per the operator's content engine, every triggered post gets a unique 4-8 character trigger.

## Common imitation failures

When ghostwriters copy this pattern, the most common failures:

- **Padding the list to hit a number.** If the operator only has 7 strong principles, ship 7. Stretching to 10 with two weak items breaks the credibility-per-item rate and the held-back item (item 10) lands soft because items 8-9 already signaled the operator was reaching.
- **Putting the held-back item at position 5 or 6 instead of the end.** The held-back-item promise creates tension that holds dwell through the early items. If item 5 is the "most important one," items 6-10 deflate. The held-back item must land at the bottom of the list.
- **Generic principles.** "Be authentic" and "tell stories" do not earn a screenshot. The principle has to carry a specific mechanism or a specific number. Ghostwriters who fall back on generic LinkedIn-coach platitudes produce posts that get ~3K impressions despite the formula.
- **Selling the doc inside the list.** "Item 5: use specific numbers — like the ones in my upcoming doc" breaks the value-stream. The doc-pitch comes after item 10, not inside the list.
- **Reusing the trigger word across posts.** "Comment 'PLAYBOOK'" used three times in three months drops responsiveness 60-70%. Unique trigger per post.
- **Not pre-building the doc.** The "I built a 24-page document" line creates an implicit promise. If the doc doesn't exist when the comment volume hits, the operator either delivers a thin doc (breaks trust) or delays delivery (kills the funnel). The doc must exist before the post ships.
- **Building the doc but skipping the qualification follow-up.** The CTA delivers the doc to the commenter; the qualification follow-up ("which of the 10 are you struggling with most?") is what converts the magnet-recipient into a discovery-call prospect. Operators who deliver the doc and stop there see 8-15% magnet-to-call conversion. Operators who add the one-question follow-up see 25-35%. The follow-up question is the bridge.
- **Letting the principles list contradict the operator's own prior content.** A common ghostwriter failure: item 6 ("name the mechanism") contradicts an earlier post where the operator argued against named-frameworks. Audiences notice. Before shipping, audit the principles against the operator's last 30-day content for consistency. Inconsistency on principle-list posts depresses long-term authority more than it lifts the single post.

## Adaptation notes (different ICP / pillar)

- **Different ICP:** the structure transfers to any operator who can credibly enumerate principles in their domain. Examples: "11 patterns I noticed running cold-email ops for 23 SaaS companies" (B2B sales); "7 moves that turn a discovery call from a 12% close to a 28% close" (consultative sales); "13 principles for hiring your first 5 engineers without a recruiter" (founder ops). The formula is invariant; the principles change.
- **Different pillar:** this format is Authority-pure with an Offer-bridge close. Pure-Authority variant: drop the comment-trigger CTA and the lead-magnet pitch — the post becomes a 10-principle prescriptive listicle that builds authority but doesn't capture leads. Pure-Offer-bridge variant: shorten the principle list to 5-6 items and dedicate more body real estate to the lead magnet pitch and the qualification language. Pier-tilted variant: make item 10 a contrarian provocation that invites debate (e.g., "principle 10: most LinkedIn coaches are wrong about reply rate, here's why") — this lifts comment volume but adds defensive comment-replies to the operational load.
- **Different scope (agency vs. client):** this format works on agency content because the operator owns the principles and the lead magnet. On client content, only ship if the client has both (a) genuine expertise to enumerate the principles, and (b) infrastructure to deliver the magnet (manychat or manual-send capacity). Per `INVARIANTS.md` N-1, never use agency-built lead magnets on a client account.
- **Different phase:** in Phase 1 (sub-5K followers, per pillar-architecture.md Rule 2), this format is high-leverage but the lead-magnet conversion will be 30-50% lower because the audience hasn't built enough trust with the operator to download. Run the format anyway; the doc downloads in Phase 1 are the highest-quality leads (audience members who actually intend to act). In Phase 3 (25K+ followers), the format saturates faster — the audience has seen the operator's principle-list posts before, so vary the principle-domain (move from writing principles to algorithm principles to comment-reply principles) to refresh.

## Operational note: the cross-post compound multiplier

The single largest performance lift on this format is the cross-post compound — not the original post itself. The carousel summary on day 3 added ~32K impressions; the talking-head video on day 5 added ~18K. Each follow-up format references the original post in the comments, which pulls audience members back to the original post and lifts its second-week impressions by ~15%.

Operators who ship the principle-list post and don't build the carousel + video follow-ups capture ~60% of the format's total impression potential. The cross-post compound is not optional for this format; it's part of the format. Build the carousel and the video script before shipping the original post — the production load is ~2-3 hours total, and the impression lift is 1.7-2.0x.

The sequence: Day 1 text post → Day 3 carousel summary (3-5 slides, one principle per slide for the top 3-5 principles, doc-CTA on the final slide) → Day 5 video (60-90 second face-to-camera on the held-back principle, with the same doc-CTA in the post copy). Each format reaches a different segment of the audience and the algorithm reads them as related (engagement compounds).

## The structural blueprint (extract for use)

```
[HOOK — List-promise, specific number + specific outcome]

[HELD-BACK ITEM TEASE — "The (N+1)th one is the most important — and almost nobody runs it"]

[NUMBERED LIST — N items, each 25-35 words, structure: claim → why → prescription, at least one specific multiplier embedded]

[HELD-BACK ITEM AT POSITION N+1 — Different category from items 1-N (e.g., operations vs. craft); ties together the prior items as a unified system]

[DOC-PITCH — "I built a {N}-page document with the worked examples for each of these — {what's inside}"]

[COMMENT-TRIGGER CTA — Unique trigger word, 4-8 chars, never reused across posts]

[QUALIFICATION-IN-PERSONALITY — Parenthetical that filters tire-kickers (e.g., "Send manually so I can see who's actually shipping")]
```
