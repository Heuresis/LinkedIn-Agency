# Authority — Contrarian Reframe — SaaS Positioning

> **Source profile:** B2B SaaS founder operating in the developer-tools vertical. Public account, ~28K followers at time of post. Anonymized per workspace canon.
>
> **Performance signals:** ~74K impressions, ~640 reactions, ~110 substantive comments, ~50 reshares, ~280 profile-views in the 72 hours after publish. Author publicly attributed three discovery calls and one $24K closed deal to the post within 30 days.

## The post

```
Most B2B SaaS founders try to write LinkedIn content that explains what their product does.

The ones who get inbound calls write content that explains what their buyer is afraid of.

I learned this the expensive way.

Spent 6 months writing posts about our product features. Average impressions: 1,400. Calls from LinkedIn: zero.

Then I rewrote one post about a fear our buyer has — the fear that they're going to ship a feature that breaks production at 2am on a Saturday and they'll be the one who gets paged.

Same audience.
Same product.
Same writing time per post.

That post hit 47K impressions. Six discovery calls in the next two weeks. Two of them closed.

Here's what I think is happening.

Your buyer is not actually thinking about your product when they scroll LinkedIn at 9pm on a Tuesday. They're thinking about the thing that's keeping them up at night.

If your post talks about your product, you're competing with their dinner.

If your post talks about the thing keeping them up at night, you're sitting next to them in the room where they're already worrying.

The post that converts is the post they were already writing in their head.

The product post is the post you wanted to write.

These are not the same job.
```

## Why it worked

**Hook formula used.** Contrarian-reframe (formula #1 in `hook-anatomy.md`).
- "Most {role}s do X. The ones who win do Y."
- The reader's pattern-match expectation: "another LinkedIn tip about content." Reality: a stake about who's wrong AND why.

**Hook scoring (against the 5-axis rubric):**
- Stop-the-scroll: 5 (clean contrarian frame, specific role)
- Specificity: 4 (B2B SaaS founders is specific enough, "what their product does" is concrete)
- Voice match: 5 (assumes voice profile fit, but the structure carries operator regardless)
- Promise alignment: 5 (body delivers the contrast claim with stakes)
- Filter strength: 4 (filters out non-B2B-SaaS readers gracefully)

**Body mechanics:**
- Single-sentence paragraphs throughout (dwell-time engineering)
- Open loop in middle ("Here's what I think is happening")
- Specific numbers as credibility currency (1,400 impressions; 47K impressions; six calls; two closes)
- Counter-conventional truth statement ("you're competing with their dinner") — landed as quotable
- No CTA — the implied action is "comment with your take," which drove the comment volume

**Virality mechanism leveraged:**
- **Pier mechanism** — published in a window where another high-engagement SaaS-founder post was running; this post landed in the same audience's feed within 18 hours
- **Dwell-time engineering** — line breaks every 8-12 words, single-sentence paragraphs, narrative loop in middle. Estimated dwell ~14 seconds (vs. typical 4-7s for B2B posts)
- **Comment-reply harvest** — author replied to every comment in the first 90 minutes, then a second wave at 6-8 hours. Doubled comment depth.

## The structural blueprint (extract for use)

```
[HOOK — Contrarian-reframe, specific role + specific common-vs-contrarian practice]

[STAKE LINE — What changes when you do the contrarian thing]

[CREDIBILITY OPENER — "I learned this the expensive way" / "I figured this out by failing for {time}"]

[THE BEFORE — Specific failure data with numbers]

[THE PIVOT MOMENT — Single sentence describing the change]

[THE AFTER — Specific success data with numbers]

[THE MECHANISM — "Here's what I think is happening." Then the explanation.]

[THE QUOTABLE TRUTH — One short paragraph that's screenshot-worthy]

[THE LANDING — One sharp closing sentence that contrasts the two paths]
```

## What NOT to copy

- The specific numbers (1,400 → 47K, $24K closed deal). These are this operator's honest data. Substitute the subject's real numbers; never invent.
- The specific fear ("ship a feature that breaks production at 2am Saturday"). This is dev-tools-vertical-specific. Substitute the subject's actual ICP fear from their `voice_of_customer.fears`.
- The "I learned this the expensive way" credibility opener if the subject has no actual prior failure to cite. Use a different credibility anchor.
