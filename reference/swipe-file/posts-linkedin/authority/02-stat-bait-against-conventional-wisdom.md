# Authority — Stat-Bait + Contrarian-Frame — Against Conventional Wisdom

> **Source profile:** B2B SaaS founder, ~31K followers at time of post. Operates publicly in the product-led-growth vs. sales-led-growth debate. Anonymized per workspace canon.
>
> **Performance signals:** ~112K impressions, ~890 reactions, ~210 substantive comments, ~95 reshares. Author publicly attributed 14 inbound demos and 4 closed deals (mid-five-figure ACV) to the post over the next 30 days. The post was reshared into 3 different SaaS-founder Slack communities, which seeded a second wave of impressions on day 4-6.

## The post

```
68% of B2B SaaS founders still believe product-led growth means "let the product sell itself."

We pulled the data on 230 PLG-claiming companies last month.

The ones above $10M ARR don't run PLG. They run a hybrid that looks like PLG from the outside and runs like sales-led-growth on the inside.

Here's what they're actually doing — and why the "pure PLG" thesis breaks above $5M ARR.

ONE: their free tier exists, but 84% of revenue comes from accounts that talked to a human in the first 30 days.

TWO: they staff "product-led sales" reps whose job is to identify high-intent free users inside the product, then trigger a human-led conversation. Average time from signup to first sales touch in this cohort: 11 days.

THREE: pricing pages show self-serve tiers, but 71% of >$25K ACR contracts are negotiated, not list-price. The published pricing exists to anchor, not to transact.

FOUR: their content is not "product education." It's "buyer education for the procurement committee" — security docs, ROI calculators, integration playbooks. None of it is what most PLG advocates write.

FIVE: they hire engineers as their first 10 sales hires. Not classic SDRs. Engineers who can do the technical scoping a procurement-driven buyer needs by call 2.

SIX: their NPS-led "viral loop" is real, but it's loop-3, not loop-1. The first sale is sales-led. The second user inside that account is product-led. The expansion is product-led. The new logo is sales-led again.

SEVEN: the founders publicly call themselves PLG because that's the narrative the market rewards. Internally, they call themselves "buyer-led with a self-serve front door."

The thing that breaks people's mental model: pure PLG works at the bottom of the market and inside engineering-tools verticals. Above $5M ARR in any non-engineer-buyer category, the math stops working — CAC payback stretches past 18 months, expansion stalls because there's no human relationship to upsell into, and the founder has to either rebuild the GTM or sell to a strategic.

The "PLG vs. SLG" debate is about 6 years out of date.

The actual debate is "what's the right hybrid for this category, this ACR, this buyer." And the answer is almost never the pure version of either.
```

## Why this worked

### Hook anatomy (per /score-hook 5-axis rubric)

- **Specificity: 5** — "68% of B2B SaaS founders" + "230 PLG-claiming companies" stacks two specific numbers in the first 25 words. Specificity is the credibility lock for stat-bait hooks; without it, the brain processes the post as opinion rather than research.
- **Stake: 5** — the implicit stake is "the thing you publicly believe and are building around is wrong." Direct threat to the reader's current operating model. High-stake content has higher dwell because the reader needs to know whether to update their thesis.
- **Pattern-break: 5** — most PLG-debate posts ARGUE the position. This one cites a sample size and reframes the entire debate as obsolete. The pattern-break is "I'm not in the debate, I'm calling the debate dead."
- **Voice-fit: 4** — assumes the operator has standing to make this claim (data, public position in the debate, prior posts establishing they look at the category empirically). Without that standing, the claim reads as posturing.
- **Decay-resistance: 4** — stat-bait hooks with proprietary-feeling numbers ("we pulled the data on 230 companies") decay slower than rhetorical hooks because the audience treats them as research, not opinion. Will hold for 18-24 months in this format before pattern fatigue.

### Body construction (mechanism + proof + specificity)

- **The contrarian frame is in the third sentence, not the first.** The first sentence sets the stat. The second establishes proprietary data. The third drops the contrarian thesis. This three-beat structure is the dwell-engineered version of "stat-bait + reframe" — most operators put the contrarian claim in line 1 and lose the credibility anchor.
- **Seven numbered points, each with a specific number.** "84% of revenue from accounts that talked to a human." "11 days from signup to first sales touch." "71% of >$25K ACR contracts negotiated." Each point lands an empirical claim, not a rhetorical one. The numbered structure also creates a list-promise sub-mechanism inside the post — readers know there are exactly seven items, which lifts read-through.
- **The "what they actually call themselves internally" line (point 7) is the narrative dagger.** It implies that the public PLG narrative is theater — which is the move that earns the reshare. Reshares happen when the reader thinks "this confirms a suspicion I already had but couldn't articulate."
- **The "thing that breaks people's mental model" reframe before the closer is the dwell-extender.** Reader has just consumed seven specific points; the reframe gives them the synthesis. Without the synthesis paragraph, the post reads as a listicle and bounces. With it, the post reads as research with a thesis.

### Virality mechanism triggered

- **Dwell-time engineering (mechanism #2 from virality-engine.md).** Single-sentence paragraphs throughout. Numbered list breaks up the body. Two-line synthesis paragraph before the closer. Estimated dwell ~22 seconds (vs. typical 4-7s for B2B posts; the post is in the top decile for dwell within authority-stat-bait category).
- **Pier mechanism (mechanism #1).** The post landed during a week when two other PLG-vs-SLG debate posts were running in the same operator-cluster. The author commented substantively on both within the first 6 hours of THIS post going live. Cross-traffic from those comments pulled an additional ~14K impressions over days 2-3.
- **Comment-reply harvest (mechanism #3).** Author replied to every commenter in the first 90 minutes with a follow-up data point or a clarifying nuance. This is the move that converts skeptical commenters into reshare-rs — they came in to push back, the author engaged with substance, they reshared with their own commentary as a "this changed my mind" post.

### CTA construction

The post has **no explicit CTA**. The implied CTA is "DM me if you want the underlying dataset" — and ~40 of the substantive comments asked for the data, which the author fulfilled via DM with a 1-pager that included a soft offer to a 30-min consult. The lack of explicit CTA is structural: stat-bait posts that end with "comment for the data" read as engagement-bait and depress reshare rate. The implicit-CTA pattern preserves credibility while still routing high-intent commenters into a DM funnel.

## Common imitation failures

When ghostwriters copy this pattern, the most common failures:

- **Inventing the stat.** "68%" and "230 companies" are the credibility lock. If the operator doesn't have proprietary data (or a citable third-party source), the entire post collapses. Ghostwriters who fake the stat get caught when commenters ask for methodology, and the operator's authority takes a 6-month hit. Per `INVARIANTS.md` N-3 — never invent results.
- **Using a contrarian frame the operator can't defend in comments.** The contrarian frame is the highest-leverage line in the post AND the highest-risk. If the operator can't defend "pure PLG breaks above $5M ARR" in 200 substantive comments with nuance, the comments turn into a credibility audit and the post backfires. Rule: the operator must have at minimum 5 prior posts establishing the position, or 3 podcast appearances on the topic, or direct operating experience that backs the claim.
- **Stretching the list past 7 items.** Seven items is at the upper bound of what readers will work through in a single dwell session. Ghostwriters who stretch to 10-12 items break dwell — readers skim the back half and engagement drops. If the post genuinely has 10+ points, split into a part-1/part-2 cross-post compound (per virality-engine.md mechanism #4).
- **Skipping the synthesis paragraph before the closer.** The "thing that breaks people's mental model" line is what converts a listicle into a thesis. Ghostwriters who omit it leave the post feeling unfinished — the reader gets seven facts but no reason to act or reshare.
- **Adding an explicit CTA at the end.** "Comment 'PLG' for the dataset" turns the post into an offer-bridge post and depresses the reshare rate by 40-60%. Stat-bait + contrarian-frame posts work because they read as research, not as marketing. Adding a CTA breaks the frame.
- **Front-loading the contrarian frame in line 1.** A common ghostwriter instinct is to lead with the most provocative line. For stat-bait posts, this inverts the credibility-stack: the reader gets the claim before the data, processes it as opinion, and bounces. The structure that works is stat → proprietary-data anchor → contrarian thesis. Three sentences, three beats, in that order.
- **Hedging the numbered points.** "Roughly 80% of revenue" and "approximately 11 days" depress the credibility-per-point rate. If the operator has the data, the data is the data — quote it precisely. If the operator only has rough estimates, ship a different post format (Authority-mechanism, not stat-bait) that doesn't require precision.
- **Padding the closer with summary or thanks.** "What do you think?" / "Hope this was useful" / "Drop your thoughts below" depresses the reshare rate by 25-40%. Stat-bait posts close cleanly on the synthesis paragraph and the final reframe — anything after that reads as content-marketing throat-clearing.

## Adaptation notes (different ICP / pillar)

- **Different ICP:** the structure transfers cleanly to any debate where the operator has proprietary data or strong empirical standing. Examples: "73% of agency founders still believe X about pricing" (B2B services); "61% of HR-tech buyers still believe Y about applicant tracking" (HR tech); "82% of devops teams still believe Z about observability tooling" (developer tools). The hook formula is invariant; the data and the contrarian thesis change.
- **Different pillar:** this post is Authority-pure. Adapting to Proof: anchor the reframe in a single specific client transformation that proves the contrarian point (see file 11). Adapting to Pier: shift the contrarian thesis to be more provocative — "PLG is dead" rather than "PLG is misunderstood" — and accept that the post will earn 50% more impressions but 40% more hostile comments. Adapting to Personality: shift the credibility anchor from "we pulled the data on 230 companies" to "I made this exact mistake at our company for 18 months and here's what it cost us" — the structure preserves but the standing changes from researcher to practitioner.
- **Different scope (agency vs. client):** this format is high-leverage on agency content because the operator usually has the dataset. On client content, only ship this format if the client has the proprietary data or third-party citation — never invent on a client's behalf. Per `INVARIANTS.md` N-1 (client voice load-bearing) and N-3 (never invent results), the data anchor must be real on either scope.
- **Different awareness state:** this format works best on a solution-aware ICP (per pillar-architecture.md Rule 3) — the audience already knows the category exists and is comparing approaches. On a problem-aware ICP, the contrarian thesis lands as confusion (the reader doesn't have enough context to evaluate the claim); shift to a Personality-anchored version that builds the context first. On a most-aware ICP, the post lands well but is over-engineered for that audience; a shorter Authority-mechanism post will produce equivalent inbound at lower operator-cost.

## Operational note: the data-prep load

Stat-bait + contrarian-frame posts require the operator to have the underlying dataset accessible during the comment-reply window. Commenters will ask for methodology, sample composition, geographic scope, ACR breakdown — and "let me get back to you" replies depress the comment-thread momentum within 3-4 hours. The operator should have a 1-2 page methodology doc ready to paste into DMs, plus a willingness to share aggregate (anonymized) data on request.

The pattern that converts the post into pipeline is "operator replies to substantive methodology questions with the actual data within 30 minutes." The pattern that burns the post is "operator goes silent for 8 hours after the methodology questions start, then replies with marketing-deflection." Plan the publish window around the 4-hour active-defense block.

## The structural blueprint (extract for use)

```
[HOOK — Stat-bait, specific percentage + specific population descriptor]

[CREDIBILITY ANCHOR — "We pulled the data on N {entities}" or "Across N {data points}"]

[CONTRARIAN THESIS — Single sentence, quantified scope, named threshold ("above $X ARR" / "in {category}")]

[DE-ESCALATION LINE — "I know how this sounds" / "Here's what they're actually doing"]

[NUMBERED LIST — 5-7 items, each with one specific number, each landing one empirical claim]

[SYNTHESIS PARAGRAPH — "The thing that breaks people's mental model: {category-wide diagnosis}"]

[CLOSER — "The {old debate} is about N years out of date. The actual debate is {reframed debate}."]
```
