# Offer-bridge — Hook-Loop-CTA — Comment-Trigger Lead Magnet

> **Source profile:** B2B agency operator, ~22K followers. Anonymized.
>
> **Performance signals:** ~38K impressions, ~290 reactions, ~340 comments (heavily driven by the trigger CTA — workspace-typical pattern for offer-bridge posts), ~14 reshares. Author attributed 47 lead-magnet downloads + 11 discovery calls + 3 retainer signs over the next 14 days.

## The post

```
I sent 3,200 cold DMs last quarter.

Reply rate: 11.4%.

Here's the part that surprised me — 73% of the replies came from messages 4-7 in the sequence, not message 1.

The standard advice on LinkedIn DM ops is "front-load your value" — make message 1 the great message and let the rest do follow-up.

That's wrong if you're trying to actually close.

Message 1 is for filtering. Most of the people who don't reply to message 1 weren't going to be a fit anyway.

Messages 2-3 are for handling the "interested but cautious" middle band — the people who saw your message, didn't reply, but didn't hide it either.

Messages 4-7 are where the closes happen. By message 4, anyone still in the thread has seen you four times in their inbox without you being annoying. That's the trust threshold.

But almost nobody runs sequences past message 3 because they don't have a structure for what to say in message 4-7 without sounding desperate.

So I built one.

It's a 7-message sequence, with the exact "what to say in each slot" framework. The framework that produced the 11.4% reply rate above (vs. industry average ~5%).

I'm sharing it as a doc — what each message does, why it works, and the actual templates with the personalization variables tagged.

Comment "DM7" and I'll send it to you.

(I'll send manually so I can see who's actually using LinkedIn DM ops vs. just curious.)
```

## Why it worked

**Hook formula used.** Specific-stakes opener (formula #2) feeding into a Hook-Loop-CTA structure.
- The opener number (3,200 DMs) is the credibility lock
- The "73% from messages 4-7" is the contrarian hook payoff

**Hook scoring:**
- Stop-the-scroll: 5 (specific volume number, specific reply rate)
- Specificity: 5 (every number is specific)
- Voice match: 4 (operator-specific data; structure carries)
- Promise alignment: 5 (body delivers the actual reframe, then offers the playbook)
- Filter strength: 5 (B2B-DM-curious self-identify; everyone else bounces)

**Body mechanics:**
- The standard-advice-then-reframe structure activates "what most people get wrong" pattern (Authority sub-mechanism in offer-bridge post)
- Sequence-position breakdown (M1 vs. M2-3 vs. M4-7) reads as proprietary IP
- The "almost nobody runs sequences past message 3 because..." is the gap statement that makes the lead magnet feel necessary
- The CTA close ("Comment 'DM7'") is high-trigger and unambiguous
- The parenthetical at the end ("I'll send manually so I can see who's actually using LinkedIn DM ops vs. just curious") is the qualification disguised as personality — filters comment-bots and tire-kickers

**Virality mechanism leveraged:**
- **Comment-trigger CTA** (mechanism #5 in `virality-engine.md`) — drove 340 comments, lifting algorithmic distribution materially
- **Comment-reply harvest** — author replied within 60 minutes to every commenter with the doc + a follow-up qualifying question
- The 47 lead-magnet downloads → 11 discovery calls = 23% conversion rate from magnet → call (high; typical is 8-15%)

## What NOT to copy

- The specific data (3,200 DMs / 11.4% reply rate). Substitute the subject's real data from outbound engine logs.
- The specific framework (7-message sequence). Substitute the subject's actual playbook — the post is a wrapper, not the content.
- The "DM7" trigger word. Pick a unique trigger per post; reusing dilutes responsiveness.
- The "I'll send manually" line if the workspace is using ManyChat for delivery. Don't lie. Pick a different qualification line.

## Operational note: ManyChat vs. manual delivery

This post used manual delivery, which capped at ~50 magnet sends in the first 24 hours. If volume is the priority, ManyChat (or equivalent) auto-delivery in <1 hour preserves trust AND scales. The "I'll send manually" line creates intimacy but limits scale; pick the trade explicitly.
