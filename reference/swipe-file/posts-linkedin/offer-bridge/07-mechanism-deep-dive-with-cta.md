# Offer-bridge — Mechanism Deep-Dive with Comment-Trigger CTA

> **Source profile:** B2B agency operator selling DFY (done-for-you) LinkedIn-content service. ~17K followers at time of post. Mature offer, ~3 years operating. Anonymized per workspace canon.
>
> **Performance signals:** ~58K impressions (band: 40K-80K), ~340 reactions, ~280 comments (most driven by the trigger CTA), ~22 reshares. Author attributed 71 lead-magnet sends + 14 discovery calls + 4 retainer signs ($9-12K/mo each) over the next 21 days.

## The post

```
We use the 3-Layer Hook Pyramid to take a client's content from "no inbound" to "5+ qualified DMs per week" inside 60 days.

Here's the 3-step version. (Comment "PYRAMID" and I'll send the full 14-page breakdown with the calibration worksheet.)

Layer 1 — The Stake Layer.

This is the first 7-12 words. The job is one job: make the right reader stop scrolling and the wrong reader keep going.

Most agencies write hooks that work for everyone. That's the problem. A hook that everyone clicks is a hook that nobody converts on.

The Stake Layer names a specific role plus a specific stake. "Most B2B SaaS founders writing LinkedIn content explain what their product does. The ones who get inbound calls explain what their buyer is afraid of." Stops the right reader. Filters the wrong one.

Layer 2 — The Mechanism Layer.

The middle 60-70% of the post. Most posts go straight to "here's what to do." The Mechanism Layer goes to "here's WHY most people get this wrong, and the specific structural reason your version isn't working."

This is where authority is built. Anyone can ship tactics. The operator who can name the mechanism behind the tactic is the one the buyer trusts to actually fix the problem.

Layer 3 — The Bridge Layer.

The last 100-200 words. This is where the post turns the reader's "interesting" into "I want what this person sells."

Most posts skip this entirely or dump a "DM me" CTA at the end. The Bridge Layer sets up a specific next step that the reader self-selects into. A trigger word, a specific binary question, an invitation to a free asset that only the right buyer wants.

The Pyramid is built bottom-up: Mechanism Layer first (the IP), then Stake Layer (the entry), then Bridge Layer (the exit).

Built top-down (hook first, mechanism after), it produces engagement-bait posts that don't convert.

Built bottom-up, it produces the posts that book calls.

Comment "PYRAMID" below and I'll send you the full 14-page breakdown — the worksheet, the 9 hook formulas keyed to each layer, and the 4-call-per-week calibration table.

(I send manually + via auto-DM, depending on volume. If you don't see the doc within an hour, ping me and I'll resend.)
```

## Why this worked

**Hook formula used.** Named-mechanism (formula #3 in `hook-anatomy.md`).
- "The 3-Layer Hook Pyramid" — proprietary-feeling name + specific outcome ("no inbound" → "5+ qualified DMs/week")
- Specific time anchor (60 days) creates believability without inflating

**Hook scoring (against the 5-axis rubric):**
- Stop-the-scroll: 5 (named mechanism + specific outcome contrast)
- Specificity: 5 (every claim has a number; "60 days," "5+ DMs/week," "14-page breakdown")
- Voice match: 4 (depends on operator voice; the structure carries)
- Promise alignment: 5 (body delivers the actual mechanism, three named layers)
- Filter strength: 5 (B2B-content-curious self-identify; everyone else bounces)

**Body mechanics:**
- Three named layers (Stake / Mechanism / Bridge) — naming creates IP feel; numbered + named is the heavy positioning move
- Each layer follows the same internal shape: name → job → why most people get it wrong → the contrarian structural answer
- The "built bottom-up vs. top-down" reframe in the closing third is the contrarian-truth payoff that makes the framework feel proprietary
- The CTA paragraph is two-part: trigger word + "what specifically you'll get" (worksheet, 9 formulas, calibration table) — overdelivers in the description so the comment cost feels low
- The parenthetical disclosure ("I send manually + via auto-DM, depending on volume") is honesty as personality — establishes operator credibility AND manages expectations

**Virality mechanism leveraged:**
- **Comment-trigger CTA** (mechanism #5 in `virality-engine.md`) — drove 280 comments, lifting algorithmic distribution materially
- **Dwell-time engineering** — single-sentence paragraphs throughout, line-break density high, three named layers create internal narrative arc
- **Comment-reply harvest** — author replied to every commenter within 90 minutes with the magnet + a single qualifying question ("what's your current avg DM volume per week from content?")
- The Class M sequence kicks in immediately — auto-DM fires the magnet within 1 hour, Day 2 sends consumption check, Day 4 sends magnet-to-call bridge

## Common imitation failures

- **Inventing the mechanism name.** "The 3-Layer Hook Pyramid" only works because this operator actually USES this framework with clients. A made-up name with no actual delivery system underneath produces a one-time engagement spike then erodes trust on the second try.
- **Three layers that aren't actually distinct.** Operators copy the structure but the three "layers" are redundant or overlap. Each layer must do a different job — see how this post defines distinct jobs (filter / authority-build / call-book).
- **CTA without the over-built description.** "Comment PYRAMID and I'll DM you" works ~3x worse than "Comment PYRAMID and I'll send you the 14-page breakdown with the worksheet, the 9 hook formulas, and the calibration table." Specificity in the magnet description = comment volume.
- **Trigger word that's not unique.** Re-using "GUIDE" or "PLAYBOOK" across multiple posts dilutes the CTA — readers stop noticing. Unique trigger per post.
- **Skipping the parenthetical honesty.** The "I send manually + via auto-DM" line manages a real operator failure mode (delays kill conversion). Skipping it produces a wave of "where's the doc?" comments that the operator has to manage manually anyway.

## Adaptation notes

- **Substitute the operator's actual named mechanism.** Don't run this post with a fabricated name. Pull from `business_context.named_mechanisms[*]` — only use mechanisms the operator actually delivers in client work.
- **Substitute real client outcomes for the "no inbound → 5+ DMs/week" claim.** Pull from `case_studies.agency_case_studies[*].outcomes`. Never invent.
- **Calibrate the magnet to actual depth.** The "14-page breakdown" is real for this operator. If the workspace's magnet is 4 pages, say so. Inflating page count breaks trust on delivery.
- **The trigger word should be 4-7 letters, ALL CAPS, unambiguous, and not used in the operator's last 8 posts.** Pull from a rotating word bank in the workspace's `outputs/{date}/triggers.md` log.
- **ManyChat (or equivalent) for auto-DM delivery is non-negotiable for posts above ~30 magnet sends.** Manual delivery at 71 sends = 6+ hour delays = ~40% of the magnet-to-call funnel lost.
