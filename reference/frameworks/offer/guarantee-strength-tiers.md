# Guarantee Strength Tiers

> The 5 guarantee tiers — from weakest (conditional refund) to strongest (better-than-money-back) — and the case-study density + margin headroom required to ship each one without burning the agency.

## Why this framework exists

Most LinkedIn agencies skip guarantees because they are afraid of refund exposure. The agency that ships a real guarantee differentiates instantly because the buyer reads the guarantee as a confidence signal — *"the operator believes in this transformation enough to risk money on it."* That signal closes deals at materially higher rates than no-guarantee offers.

But the wrong guarantee burns the agency. A performance guarantee shipped against a transformation the agency has only delivered twice = a fast track to a 6-month cash crisis when month 3 brings the inevitable refund cluster. A "satisfaction guaranteed" guarantee = guarantee theater that erodes trust on close calls because no one can name what would falsify it.

This framework matches guarantee strength to evidence. The tier the operator can ship is determined by case-study density (how many proven wins of this transformation type) + margin headroom (whether the agency can absorb the worst-case refund volume without cash-position damage). Shipping above your evidence band is the failure mode this framework prevents.

## The 5 tiers (weakest → strongest)

### Tier 1 — Conditional Refund

**Form.** "Refund available if {specific conditions met} within {window}."

**When appropriate.** Earliest validation tier. ≤3 case studies in this transformation type, OR the operator has just launched the offer and is still proving the wedge. Also appropriate when the offer has high upfront fixed costs (e.g., a custom voice extraction or a vertical-specific lead-list build) and the conditions function as a "did the buyer hold up their side" filter.

**Cost to operator.** Lowest. The conditions filter out most refund requests structurally — only buyers who actually ship their side of the engagement and still see no result claim refunds. Practical refund rate: 1-3% of contracts.

**Case-study density required.** ≥1 proven case (the absolute minimum to ship any offer at all). Below 1 = no offer, no guarantee, run more pilots.

**Anti-abuse mechanics.** Conditions must be objective and easy to verify (e.g., "client attended ≥3 of 4 monthly strategy calls" / "client approved posts within 5 business days during the contract term"). Subjective conditions ("client engaged genuinely") are abuse magnets.

**Terms-and-conditions copy template.**
> "If, at the end of {window}, you have completed {specific client-side commitments — listed} and we have not delivered {specific deliverables at quality bar — listed}, you may request a refund of the most recent month's fee within 14 days of the window close."

### Tier 2 — Performance-Based

**Form.** "Refund of {amount — typically last 30 days} if {specific metric} not hit by {specific date}."

**When appropriate.** Medium evidence tier. ≥3 case studies of this transformation type with median time-to-result inside the guarantee window. Operator has confidence in the typical case but does not yet have enough volume to guarantee the worst case. Standard for offers in cycles 2-3 of the agency's growth (offer is proven but volume is still moderate).

**Cost to operator.** Moderate. Practical refund rate: 5-12% of contracts depending on transformation difficulty and ICP-fit precision. Margin must absorb 12% refund without cash damage — this implies ≥70% gross margin on the offer (per `retainer-architecture.md` Rule 8).

**Case-study density required.** ≥3 proven cases (matches the `transformation-hypothesis.md` Rule 3 threshold for shipping a standard-tier hypothesis publicly).

**Anti-abuse mechanics.** The metric must be sourced from a place the agency has visibility into (not pure client self-report). The deadline must include a reasonable client-availability assumption (e.g., "within 90 days, assuming client maintains the contractually-agreed weekly review cadence; otherwise the deadline extends day-for-day"). The refund applies to a defined window (last 30 days), not the entire contract.

**Terms-and-conditions copy template.**
> "If you have not hit {metric, defined precisely} within {N days} of contract start, AND you have maintained the contractually agreed {client-side commitments — listed}, we refund the fee of the last 30 days within 14 days of your written request."

### Tier 3 — Results-or-Work-Free (Continue Working)

**Form.** "If {specific metric} not hit by {specific date}, we continue working at no charge until you hit it."

**When appropriate.** High evidence tier. ≥5 case studies with worst-case time-to-result still inside a workable extension window. The operator is confident the transformation will land — the only question is timing. Typical for offers in cycle 3+ when the agency has dialed in the methodology and the failure cases are timing-driven (slower-than-typical buyer onboarding, slower-than-typical content engine ramp), not methodology-driven.

**Cost to operator.** Variable. Practical "extension month" rate: 8-15% of contracts. Each extension costs the operator the loaded fulfillment cost of one additional month — which the agency must price into the offer. Margin must absorb 15% extension without damage — this implies ≥75% gross margin on the offer.

**Case-study density required.** ≥5 proven cases AND the operator can name what causes timing slippage in the cases that needed an extra cycle. If the operator cannot name the timing-slippage causes, ship Tier 2 instead.

**Anti-abuse mechanics.** Set a maximum extension cap (e.g., "up to 60 additional days" — beyond that, both parties revisit the engagement). Require client to maintain client-side commitments during the extension. Document the extension trigger and the metric in the same agreement so there is no ambiguity at month 4.

**Terms-and-conditions copy template.**
> "If you have not hit {metric, defined precisely} within {N days} of contract start, AND you have maintained the contractually agreed {client-side commitments — listed}, we continue delivering the agreed scope at no additional fee until the metric hits, for up to {extension cap days} additional days."

### Tier 4 — Full Guarantee (Unconditional Refund Window)

**Form.** "{Window — typically 30-60 days} unconditional refund. No questions asked."

**When appropriate.** Mature evidence tier. ≥10 case studies of this transformation type, refund-rate data from prior unconditional offers (or comparable industry data), and operator clarity that the offer's drop-out pattern is "client was not actually serious" rather than "engagement is failing." Often shipped on the first 30 days of a longer engagement to lower the perceived close-risk for cold prospects.

**Cost to operator.** High during the refund window. Practical refund rate: 10-20% of new contracts depending on the no-questions-asked window length. The operator must have absorbed the upfront fulfillment cost (kickoff, voice extraction, ICP build, first content batch) and decided that the close-rate lift from the guarantee outweighs the refund-window losses.

**Case-study density required.** ≥10 proven cases AND a margin model that survives 20% refund rate on new contracts during the refund window.

**Anti-abuse mechanics.** Window length is the lever — 14-day windows produce minimal abuse, 60-day windows produce more. The refund applies to fees paid during the window, not to fees paid for service rendered after the window closes (this prevents "client cancels at day 89 of a 90-day contract" abuse). Some operators add a "completed onboarding" condition (client must have completed kickoff + voice extraction + ICP interview to claim refund) — this is acceptable and reduces abuse without weakening the close-side signal.

**Terms-and-conditions copy template.**
> "Within the first {N days} of your contract, you may request a full refund of fees paid during this window for any reason. Refund processed within 14 days of written request. Fees for service periods after the {N}-day window are not subject to this guarantee."

### Tier 5 — Better-Than-Money-Back

**Form.** "Refund + {bonus deliverable / extended service / payment}." Examples: "Full refund AND we keep delivering for 30 more days" / "Full refund AND we hand over the lead list + content library we built for you" / "Full refund AND a $500 payment for your time."

**When appropriate.** Highest evidence tier. ≥15 case studies, sustained low refund rate on prior tiers, and a deliberate market-signal play. Used when the agency wants to differentiate against a noisy competitive market by signaling absolute confidence and is willing to spend on the signaling.

**Cost to operator.** Highest in the worst case. Practical refund-plus-bonus rate: 6-15% (lower than Tier 4 because the offer attracts more serious buyers — the asymmetry of the guarantee filters bad-fit). Margin must absorb 15% worst-case at the bonus-inclusive cost — this implies ≥80% gross margin AND the bonus deliverable cost must be pre-modeled into the price.

**Case-study density required.** ≥15 proven cases AND prior-tier refund-rate data showing the agency can predict refund volume within ±5%. Without that prediction precision, the agency cannot price the bonus into the offer correctly.

**Anti-abuse mechanics.** The bonus deliverable should be something genuinely useful but with a low marginal cost to the operator (e.g., handing over the lead list + content library that was already built and would otherwise just be archived). Avoid bonuses that require additional fulfillment work post-refund. Same window/condition mechanics as Tier 4.

**Terms-and-conditions copy template.**
> "Within the first {N days} of your contract, you may request a full refund of fees paid during this window for any reason. In addition to the refund, we will {specific bonus deliverable — listed}. Refund and bonus processed within 14 days of written request."

## Tier selection logic

```yaml
guarantee_tier_selection:
  case_study_count: 0                     # Proven cases of this transformation type
  gross_margin_pct: 0.0                   # Per offer
  prior_refund_rate_data: yes | no        # Have data from prior tier?
  worst_case_refund_rate_estimate: 0.0    # Operator's honest worst-case
  margin_post_refund: 0.0                 # gross_margin × (1 - worst_case_refund)
  recommended_tier: 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5
  rationale: ""
```

Decision tree:
- ≥1 case + uncertain margin → **Tier 1**
- ≥3 cases + ≥70% margin → **Tier 2**
- ≥5 cases + ≥75% margin + operator can name timing-slip causes → **Tier 3**
- ≥10 cases + ≥75% margin + ≤25% worst-case refund estimate → **Tier 4**
- ≥15 cases + ≥80% margin + prior-tier refund data within ±5% prediction → **Tier 5**

If margin or case-count fails the tier requirement, ship the highest tier the operator does qualify for. Never ship above the qualifying tier "to look bold."

## Failure modes

| Failure | Symptom | Root cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|---|
| Guarantee theater ("satisfaction guaranteed") | Buyers ignore the guarantee on close calls | Operator afraid to promise outcome | Demote to Tier 1 conditional refund with named conditions; ship something falsifiable even if narrow |
| Refund cluster at month 3 | Cash-position damage in first cycle of new offer | Shipped above evidence band (e.g., Tier 3 with 2 cases) | Halt new sales of the offer; demote to Tier 1 or Tier 2; rebuild case-study density |
| Performance guarantee with subjective metric | Disputes at refund time | Metric not falsifiable per `transformation-hypothesis.md` Rule 4 | Restate the metric with measurement source; if it can't be restated falsifiably, the offer isn't ready for Tier 2+ |
| Tier 3 extension blowout (clients claim 6+ months) | Margin destruction | No cap or weak client-side conditions | Add extension cap (60 days max); require client commitments during extension |
| Tier 4 abuse — clients cancel at day 89 of 90-day window | Refund applied to fully-rendered service | Window mechanics weak | Restrict refund to fees paid during the window only, not to past-window service |
| Tier 5 bonus deliverable costs more than expected | Loss-per-refund higher than modeled | Bonus requires post-refund fulfillment | Switch to bonuses with zero marginal post-refund cost (e.g., assets already built) |
| Margin math wasn't run | Agency hits cash crisis 6 months after offer launch | Skipped honesty check | Run `retainer-architecture.md` Rule 8 honesty check before any guarantee tier ship |
